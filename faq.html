Eligibility & submitting

Any director aged 65 or older at the time of submission — that's our only hard eligibility rule. No prior festival experience, no professional credentials, no minimum budget required. In the case of co-direction, the lead director must meet this requirement.

Not strictly. We give priority to films that highlight older adult perspectives, explore intergenerational relationships, or challenge stereotypes of aging — but this isn't a disqualifying requirement. See the full breakdown of each category on our Submit page. A film whose only connection to senior experience is the director's age is unlikely to be selected, though. Not sure if yours qualifies? Submit anyway and explain the connection in your director's statement.

Fees, feedback & recognition

Really. SIAFF is self-funded with no institutional sponsors or industry ties, and submitting has always been free — including for 2027. If our work resonates with you, we gratefully welcome a donation of any amount to help cover festival costs, but it is never required.

Selected films and category winners receive written jury feedback, and every filmmaker who submits is notified of the outcome. We believe the act of making a film deserves acknowledgement — not silence. As we grow, so will our feedback capacity for every entrant.

Selected films receive a digital laurel to display freely on posters, websites, and social media. Category winners receive their own distinct laurel recognizing the award.

About SIAFF

No — by choice. SIAFF has no institutional sponsors and no ties to the film industry. We're run by the AGOAC Filmmaking Group, a community of senior filmmakers in Markham, Ontario. We do welcome collaboration with organizations that share our values. Read more about why we've stayed independent on our About page.

SIAFF was founded in Markham, Ontario, Canada, and has run there since 2024. Starting in 2027, we're opening submissions to senior filmmakers worldwide.

In 2018, filmmaker Thomas Yee founded the AGOAC Filmmaking Group in Markham to teach older adults the complete craft of filmmaking. The weekly classroom ran for years before the group decided, in 2024, that the journey deserved a public moment — and SIAFF was born. Read the full story on our About page.
