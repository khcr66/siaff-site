Submit a film

Your film belongs here.

If you are 65 or older and you have made a short film — submit it. No fee. No prior festival experience required. Just your work.

Submission fee
Free to submit.
No exceptions.
SIAFF is self-funded with no institutional sponsors or industry ties. Submitting costs nothing for 2027 — and we intend to keep it that way. Cost should never stand between a senior filmmaker and an audience. If our work resonates with you, a donation of any amount is genuinely appreciated and goes directly to keeping the festival running.
Filmmaker feedback
Honest about what we offer.
We are a learning community and we take filmmaker feedback seriously. Selected films and category winners receive written jury feedback. Every submitter receives notification of the outcome. We will not leave you in silence — and as we grow, so will our feedback capacity.

Eligibility

01
Director age — 65 or older
The film's director must be aged 65 or older at the time of submission. In the case of co-direction, the lead director must meet this requirement. This is our only hard eligibility rule.
02
Senior-centric subject matter — strongly preferred
Films that engage meaningfully with senior experience are given priority in our selection process. This is not a disqualifying requirement — but it is what SIAFF programs. A film whose only connection to senior experience is the director's age is unlikely to be selected.
03
Short film format
Short films only. Maximum runtime 30 minutes including credits. Any genre — documentary, fiction, experimental, animation, or hybrid. Any language; non-English films must include English subtitles. No World Premiere requirement — films that have screened elsewhere are welcome.

What "senior-centric" means

A film is senior-centric when it meaningfully engages with one or more of the following:

A Highlights older adult perspectives — the film sees the world through older eyes. The senior viewpoint or life experience is the lens, not just the subject.
B Explores intergenerational relationships — what passes between generations: love, conflict, inheritance, care, legacy. Both sides of the relationship matter.
C Challenges stereotypes of aging — the film pushes back against what society expects of older people. Comedy, satire, and the defiant belong here as much as the reflective.

Not sure if your film qualifies? Submit it anyway and include a brief note in your director's statement explaining how it engages with older adult experience. We would rather consider your work and decide than have you self-select out before we see it.

What we look for

SIAFF judges films on clarity of vision relative to resources and experience — not on production budget or technical polish. A first-time filmmaker with a smartphone and something specific to say belongs in our program as much as a retired cinematographer with professional equipment.

We ask our jury: did this filmmaker achieve what they set out to achieve, given what they had? That is a different and more honest question than "is this technically accomplished?"

How to submit

SIAFF accepts submissions exclusively through FilmFreeWay — the industry-standard festival platform. Create a free filmmaker account, complete your film's profile, and submit to SIAFF. The process takes about 20 minutes.

All submissions are reviewed by the full jury. Selections are announced on the date noted in the sidebar. All filmmakers are notified of the outcome.

Selected films receive a digital laurel to display proudly on your film's materials. Category winners receive their own distinct laurel recognizing their award. These are yours to use freely — on your film poster, your website, your social media, anywhere you share your work.
